   The DRL method obtains flight paths based on the interac-
tion between the agent and the environment [22]. It is capable
of handling high-dimensional state space and action space,
which allows it to address complex scenarios [23]. An accurate
aircraft model is fundamental to the path planning method for
representing its dynamic characteristics [24]. Existing studies
on aircraft modeling that consider dynamic characteristics
can be classified into physics-based models and data-driven
models [25]. Physics-based aircraft models are established
based on the fundamental dynamic physical laws [26]. The
kinematic model and dynamic model are usually coupled to
formulate the motion state of the agent for the path planning
