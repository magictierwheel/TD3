# Hidden-Disturbance TD3 Paper Redesign

## Status

Approved research direction. This specification supersedes the legacy oracle-disturbance/PID-FF warm-start paper direction.

## Objective

Build a fair, reproducible comparison of conventional PID, end-to-end Direct TD3, and PID-based Residual TD3 for circular trajectory tracking under hidden time-varying disturbances. All controllers share observable flight-state information and none receives true environmental disturbance parameters or scenario labels.

## Scientific Claim Boundary

The revised paper may test whether a PID residual prior improves TD3 safety and sample efficiency. It must not assume that reinforcement learning guarantees stability, claim online adaptation, or claim superiority over disturbance observers, MPC, PID-FF, or real-flight controllers that are not included in the experiment.

## Controller Contract

### PID

- Inputs: reference position/velocity, measured position/velocity, attitude, angular velocity.
- Internal memory: the normal PID integrator.
- Forbidden inputs: wind, density, actuator efficiency, disturbance magnitude, or scenario label.
- Output: four motor RPM commands.

### Direct TD3

- Inputs: the same observable state/reference/error history available to Residual TD3.
- Forbidden inputs: PID command, true disturbance parameters, or scenario label.
- Output: four bounded motor RPM commands.

### Residual TD3

- Inputs: the shared observable state/reference/error history plus the PID motor command, which is a deterministic function of non-privileged information.
- Output: four bounded motor RPM deltas.
- Applied command: `clip(pid_rpm + residual_rpm, 0, max_rpm)`.
- Initialization: zero residual output so the initial controller reproduces PID.

## Information Boundary

The simulator may log disturbance truth for analysis, but controller observations, policy checkpoints, gate calculations, warm-start data, and model-selection scores must not consume it. Tests must fail if a policy observation contains disturbance fields or if a gate changes when only hidden disturbance metadata changes while observable state is held fixed.

## Safety Mechanism

Replace the legacy disturbance-magnitude gate with a gate derived only from observable tracking error, attitude/altitude safety margins, and motor headroom. Include a no-gate residual ablation. The gate is part of the residual architecture, not evidence that the policy inferred the disturbance.

## Disturbance Model

Use hidden processes rather than fixed oracle labels:

- `standard`: no disturbance.
- `random_wind`: time-varying horizontal wind, updated every 1-3 seconds or generated by a documented correlated stochastic process.
- `actuator_loss`: hidden thrust/torque efficiency variation.
- `compound`: random wind and actuator loss together.
- `unseen`: stronger amplitude and/or faster variation, never used for training or selection.

All controllers in a paired evaluation receive the exact same disturbance realization.

## Training Protocol

- Episode duration: 20 seconds, covering two nominal circular periods.
- Checkpoints: 5k, 10k, 20k, 50k, and 100k.
- Stage A: seed 0 to 20k for a go/no-go diagnostic.
- Stage B: seeds 0-2 to 50k for direction validation.
- Stage C: seeds 0-4 to 100k only if Stage B passes predeclared criteria.
- One global checkpoint budget is selected from validation aggregate results; do not select a different favorable checkpoint for each training seed.
- Validation seeds: 100-109. Test seeds: 200-219. Unseen seeds: 300-319.

## Acceptance Criteria

Before RL training, nominal PID must complete 30 seconds without failure, achieve a path-length ratio from 0.9 to 1.1, and have steady position RMSE below 0.1 m. Zero residual must match PID numerically.

At the 50k gate, Residual TD3 must improve failure rate or paired steady RMSE relative to Direct TD3 and improve compound tracking relative to PID in at least two of three training seeds without degrading standard-scene safety. Final positive claims require the same direction in at least four of five training seeds with clustered confidence intervals.

## Reporting

Report failure rate before error metrics. Separate completed-episode RMSE from failure-penalized horizon performance. Include actual flight time and never interpret early-termination RMSE as superior tracking. Aggregate training seed and disturbance seed hierarchically.

## Legacy Boundary

The existing PID-FF warm-start, oracle disturbance observation, gate-min, and 5000-step selected-checkpoint runs are archived diagnostic evidence. They explain why the study was redesigned but cannot support the revised paper's controller claims.
